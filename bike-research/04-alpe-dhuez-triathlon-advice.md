# Alpe d'Huez Triathlon (Full Ironman) — Bike Advice

The course: 118km, **3200m elevation gain**, Col de Sarenne (13km at 7%), Col d'Ornon (14km at 4%), Alpe d'Huez (13.8km, 21 bends, up to 11-13%). Then a full marathon at altitude.

---

## Official Course Recommendation

The Alpe d'Huez Triathlon organisers (France Bike Rentals) recommend:

> **"A road bike is more economical than a time trial bike. It's less demanding and lighter."**
> — Téo Katgely, triathlete & FBR e-commerce manager

**Key reasons for a road bike on this course:**

| Factor | Road Bike | TT / Tri Bike |
|---|---|---|
| **Weight** | Lighter (~8-9kg) | Heavier (~9-11kg) |
| **Climbing** | Compact crankset + wide cassette (1:1 gear) | Limited gearing (harder on 11-13% gradients) |
| **Descending** | Easier handling, safer cornering | Twitchy, more nerve-wracking |
| **Comfort** | Endurance geometry, upright position | Aggressive aero position |
| **Aero advantage** | Less aero (but 30km/h avg on climbs) | More aero on flats (~20% of course) |

**Their verdict:** Road bike + clip-on tri bars = ideal setup. TT bike only recommended for flat/hilly courses, not mountains.

## What Gearing Do You Need?

For Alpe d'Huez (11-13% gradients), the industry standard recommendation:

| Rider | Recommended low gear |
|---|---|
| Strong climber | 34/34 (1:1) — what Cube Attain has |
| Average rider | 34/36 or 34/40 |
| Light rider or first Ironman | 34/40 or 30/40 |

The Cube Attain's **50/34 + 11-34** gives 1:1 which is sufficient for most. If you want easier gears:
- Swap cassette to 11-36 (Shimano Ultegra/105 GS derailleur supports up to 36T)
- Or swap crankset to 46/33 sub-compact

**Budget for a cassette swap: ~€60-100**

## Should You Add Clip-On Tri Bars?

**Yes.** For an Ironman, the aero savings are significant even on a hilly course:

| Position | Drag (watts) | Speed at 200W |
|---|---|---|
| Drops (hoods) | ~100% baseline | 32 km/h |
| Clip-on aero bars | ~88-92% | 33-34 km/h |

On a 5-6 hour bike leg, that's **10-15 minutes saved** even with the climbs.

**Clip-on bars to consider (€50-200):**
- Profile Design T4+ / Sonic / 52SLC
- Zipp Vuka Clip
- Syncros Tri Extensions
- Redshift Quick-Release

**Must do:** Get a **professional bike fit** with the clip-ons installed. Position is critical for comfort and power over 180km.
