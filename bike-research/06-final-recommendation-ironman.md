| Bike fit (essential for tri position) | €200 |
| **Total** | **~€3,000** |

**Why NOT for Alpe d'Huez:**
- Heavier (9-10 kg) — extra 1-2 kg vs road bike uphill
- Aggressive position — hard on lower back for 5+ hours
- Limited gearing (52/36 + 11-28 common) — brutal on 13% gradients
- Rim brakes — less safe on long alpine descents
- Narrow tyres (25mm max) — less comfort and grip
- **The Alpe d'Huez organisers explicitly recommend against this**

**Only consider if:** This is your 2nd bike and you also race flat courses. Not ideal as a only bike for this race.

## What to Do Before Race Day

1. **Buy the Cube Attain C:62 SLX** from a Cube dealer in NL
2. **Install clip-on aero bars** (Profile Design, Zipp, or Syncros)
3. **Get a professional bike fit** — crucial for Ironman comfort
4. **Consider a cassette swap** to 11-36 if you're lighter or a first-timer at Alpe d'Huez
5. **Train on the setup** — get used to the aero position on climbs and descents
6. **Travel tip:** The C:62 SLX has a standard round 27.2mm seatpost and standard stem — easy to pack in a bike case/fly with
